Original Notes

Email Header & Artifact Analysis

Understanding email headers and related metadata is crucial for analyzing phishing, spoofing, and malware delivery. Below is a streamlined reference for analysts and responders.

1- Received Chain (Server Hops)

  • What it reveals: The route the email took, from originating mail server to destination.
  • Why it matters: Tracing the source IP helps validate legitimacy or expose spoofing.
  • Analyst Tip: Examine the earliest Received: entry—it’s usually closest to the attacker.
text
Received: from attacker-mail[.]net (123.45.67.89) by mail.gateway.com

2- Sender Identity Headers

From

  • Claimed sender. Can be forged easily.

Reply-To

  • Often used to redirect responses to attacker-controlled inboxes.

Return-Path

  • Bounce address. May differ from From in spoofed emails.

Investigation tip: Mismatches between these headers suggest forgery or phishing.

3- Recipient Context

To / Cc

  • Shows who the email was addressed to.

BCC (Not visible in headers)

  • Use logs to uncover others who received the same email.

4- Message Fingerprints

Message-ID

  • Globally unique. Useful for tracking campaigns or cloned messages.

Date

  • Timestamp the sender claims. Compare to your server’s timestamp for anomalies.

Thread-Index

  • Helps link replies in long phishing chains.

5- Authentication Results

Authentication-Results

  • Aggregates checks like SPF, DKIM, DMARC.

DKIM-Signature

  • Ensures content wasn’t tampered with.

Received-SPF

  • Verifies if sender IP is authorized by the domain.
jsx
Authentication-Results: dmarc=fail spf=pass dkim=pass

6- Infrastructure & Origin

X-Originating-IP

  • Original sender’s IP (when available). Useful for geolocation or abuse lookup.

X-Mailer

  • Email client or tool used to compose the email (Outlook, Thunderbird, custom scripts).

X-Received

  • Redundant hops added by large providers (e.g., Google). Helps when Received: is stripped or minimal.

7- Content Insights

Content-Type

  • Reveals if the email is plain text, HTML, or multipart.

Risk Flags:

  • text/html: Can embed phishing forms.
  • multipart/mixed: Used to deliver payloads or trick filters.
  • application/octet-stream: Often signals binary attachments (suspicious).

8- Spam & Filtering Metadata

X-Spam-Status / X-Spam-Score

  • Anti-spam engine verdicts. High scores indicate likely phishing or junk.

Investigation Checklist

ArtifactUse Case
From, Reply-ToDetect impersonation and spoofing attempts.
Received, IPs (X-Originating-IP, Received)Trace the true source and full mail path; extract sender IPs for threat intel/reputation checks.
Authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)Confirm legitimacy and detect forgery.
Content-TypeIdentify HTML or script-based payload delivery formats.
Message-IDCorrelate campaigns across recipients; detect duplicates or reused infrastructure.
Subject LineIdentify patterns in phishing lures; useful for hunting similar emails.
Attachment HashAnalyze malicious attachments using sandboxing or public threat intel platforms (e.g., VirusTotal).
URL / DomainExtract Indicators of Compromise (IOCs); check for typosquatting or domain age via WHOIS.
