TA0006Credential AccessSteal account credentials or secrets.T1003 (LSASS), T1056 (Keylogging)Credential Guard, secure LSASS configs
TA0007DiscoveryLearn about the environment and its assets.T1083 (Dir Listing), T1018 (Remote Discovery)Network segmentation, role-based access
TA0008Lateral MovementMove from one system to another internally.T1021 (Remote Services), T1210 (Exploits)Microsegmentation, monitor internal RDP/SMB
TA0009CollectionGather files, credentials, or sensitive content.T1113 (Screen Capture), T1213 (Repo Access)DLP, UEBA

Each tactic includes multiple techniques and sub-techniques. Analysts and red teamers map behavior down to the finest level for precision.

Adversary Emulation & Threat Intelligence

MITRE ATT&CK supports Adversary Emulation Plans, maintained by CTID. These simulate APT groups like APT29, FIN6, etc., and are used for purple teaming and control validation.

  • Example: Use the Navigator to map APT29’s known TTPs.
  • Leverage ATT&CK to assess threat coverage gaps in your infrastructure.
  • Prioritize real-world detection over hypothetical risk modeling.

Summary

  • Tactics are attacker objectives (e.g., Initial Access).
  • Techniques are how they achieve those objectives (e.g., Exploiting valid credentials).
  • Procedures are the real-world implementations (e.g., using Mimikatz to dump LSASS).
  • MITRE ATT&CK is central to modern threat detection, adversary emulation, and security control mapping.
