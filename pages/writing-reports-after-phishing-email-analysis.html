Overview

When analyzing a suspicious or malicious email, the first step is collecting artifacts (IOCs). These are used to correlate incidents, group emails into campaigns, identify potential threat actors, generate metrics, and monitor trends. Your report must present these clearly and concisely to support threat hunting, IOC sharing, or further investigation.

Why It Matters In SOC

Use this note as an analyst reference: understand the concept, identify the relevant telemetry, pivot through evidence, and document the decision path clearly.

  • Pivot through related notes, telemetry sources, and MITRE references.
  • Document findings, gaps, containment options, and detection improvements.

Original Notes

Writing Reports After Phishing Email Analysis

When analyzing a suspicious or malicious email, the first step is collecting artifacts (IOCs). These are used to correlate incidents, group emails into campaigns, identify potential threat actors, generate metrics, and monitor trends. Your report must present these clearly and concisely to support threat hunting, IOC sharing, or further investigation.

Email Header and Artifact Extraction

Include the following fields in your report:

Email Header Artifacts

  • Sender Address: alerts@invoice-alerts.org
  • Reply-to Address: support.billing@protonmail.com
  • Date Sent: 14th March 2025, 8:12 AM
  • Sending Server IP: 203.0.113.58
  • Reverse DNS: smtp-out123.mailrelay.example.net
  • Recipients: samantha.j@acmeindustries.com, rohit.v@acmeindustries.com
  • Subject Line: Invoice #55321 - Payment Required Immediately

URLs

  • Sanitized:

hxxps://acme-invoices-secure[.]com/portal/login/redirect.php?sessionid=99213

Attachments

  • Filename: Invoice_March2025_UK.xlsm
  • Hashes: SHA256 and MD5

Email Body Description

Attach the full email (.eml or .msg) in your case management system, along with a brief summary and (optionally) a screenshot. This helps other analysts triage without reopening the file.

Example:

The email claims to contain an overdue invoice and asks the recipient to review the file. It mimics the style of Acme’s finance system and includes a malicious Excel file with embedded macros.

Artifact Analysis

Tailor the analysis section based on the type of threat: URL or Attachment.

Malicious URLs

Include:

  • WHOIS lookup results (age, registrar, visibility)
  • VirusTotal / PhishTank / URLhaus reputation status
  • Screenshots from URL2PNG or Wannabrowser
  • Any form fields or credential traps
  • Domain behavior (e.g., no homepage or redirect chains)

Malicious Attachments

Include:

  • Hash submissions to VirusTotal / Talos
  • File type inspection (macro-embedded Office doc, script)
  • Sandbox results (Any.Run, JoeSandbox, Hybrid Analysis)
  • Observed activity:
    • Process execution
    • Network beacons
    • Registry changes
    • File system writes

Defensive Measures

List any actions taken or recommended to mitigate the threat. This could include email filters, domain blocks, file hash alerts, or subject-based rules.

Examples:

  • Subject Block: Invoice #55321 - Payment Required Immediately

Actioned by: Marcus Lee on 14 March 2025 at 09:04 AM

  • Domain Block: acme-invoices-secure[.]com

Actioned by: Marcus Lee on 14 March 2025 at 09:10 AM

IOC Defanging

To prevent accidental execution, defang all IOCs in shared reports:

  • Replace . with [.]
  • Replace http with hxxp

Examples:

  • 198.51.100.7 → 198[.]51[.]100[.]7
  • https://bad-portal.com → hxxps://bad-portal[.]com
