Original Notes

Unified Kill Chain

Overview

The Unified Kill Chain (UKC) is a robust cybersecurity framework that models the full lifecycle of modern cyberattacks — from reconnaissance to exfiltration and impact — and even attacker intent. Developed in 2017 and updated in 2022, UKC merges the Lockheed Martin Kill Chain with the MITRE ATT&CK matrix, offering granular visibility across 18 attack phases.

It emphasizes realistic attack paths, allowing for phase looping, repeated recon, and continuous privilege expansion, which makes it more adaptive than traditional static models.

Purpose & Benefits

  • Complete Lifecycle Coverage: Captures all operational phases, including overlooked areas like false flags, persistence, and pivoting.
  • Detailed Technical Mapping: Every phase is tied to MITRE ATT&CK techniques and common tools.
  • Operational Utility: Enables structured detection engineering, threat hunting, and blue/red team collaboration.
  • Realistic Attack Modeling: Accounts for recurring phases and complex attacker behavior.
  • Supports Threat Modeling: Helps map defensive gaps and evaluate incident response.

Unified Kill Chain Phases

Phases are grouped into three categories:

1- Initial Foothold

1ReconnaissanceOSINT, network scanning, employee profiling, asset discoveryTA0043 (T1592, T1589)

3DeliveryPhishing, malicious links, USB drops, file-sharingTA0001 (T1566, T1195)
4Social EngineeringUser coercion via impersonation, vishing, phishing, pretextingTA0001
5ExploitationCode execution via software flaws, misconfigurations, zero-daysTA0002 (T1190, T1211)
6PersistenceMaintain access post-reboot/session via services, registry, startup scriptsTA0003 (T1136, T1542)

#PhaseDescriptionMITRE Mapping
7Defense EvasionHide malicious activity: AV bypass, obfuscation, log cleaningTA0005 (T1562, T1027)
8Command & ControlEstablish covert channel via HTTP, DNS, peer-to-peer, encrypted protocolsTA0011 (T1071, T1095)
9PivotingUse compromised host to reach non-public internal systemsTA0008
10DiscoveryEnumerate accounts, shares, services, AD objects, subnetsTA0007 (T1018, T1083)
11Privilege EscalationElevate from user to SYSTEM, domain admin, or root via exploits/misconfigTA0004 (T1134, T1068)
12ExecutionRun malicious code: scripts, binaries, scheduled tasks, shellcodeTA0002
13Credential AccessDump passwords, harvest tokens, keylogging, phishingTA0006 (T1003, T1056)
14Lateral MovementMove across network via RDP, SMB, WMI, PsExec, or credential reuseTA0008 (T1021, T1210)
