Original Notes

Phishing Attack Techniques

1- Pretexting

  • The attacker builds a false scenario or identity to gain trust.
  • Common in HR-related or executive-targeted phishing.
  • Often combined with authority or urgency themes.

2- Spoofing & Impersonation

  • Email Spoofing: Forging the "From" field to appear legitimate.
  • Reply-To Abuse: Setting a different reply address to capture responses.
  • Domain Spoofing: Registering similar or identical-looking domains.
  • Used to impersonate coworkers, brands, executives, or vendors.

3- URL Manipulation

  • Shortening: Hides destination (e.g., Bitly, TinyURL).

  • Typosquatting: Misspelled versions of real domains.
    • Ex: securtyblue.team, securitybllue.team

  • Homoglyphs: Unicode tricks using visually similar characters.
    • Ex: gοοgle.com (Cyrillic 'o')

  • Subdomain Spoofing: paypal.com.bad-domain[.]xyz
  • Can be combined with link tracking or redirect chains.

Tools for analysis:

  • WannaBrowser
  • URL2PNG
  • Sandbox browsers

4- Encoding & Obfuscation

  • Mask malicious scripts or payloads using:
    • Base64
    • URL encoding
    • HTML entity encoding
    • Obscure JavaScript functions
  • Often seen in credential harvesters or email body source code.

5- Attachment-Based Delivery

  • Non-malicious files with social engineering messaging
  • Embedded URLs in PDFs or Office docs
  • Malicious macros in Word/Excel triggering payloads
  • Often rely on user clicking "Enable Content" to run macro

6- Abuse of Legitimate Services

  • Hosting payloads or forms on:
    • Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive
    • Mailgun, Mailchimp, Outlook, Gmail
  • Helps attackers avoid blacklists and filters
  • Often used for reputation piggybacking

7- Pharming

  • DNS-based manipulation that redirects users to fake websites
  • May involve router compromise, DNS cache poisoning
  • Victim lands on a fake site even if URL was typed correctly

8- HTML & Visual Tricks

  • Styled emails mimic brand layouts (e.g., Amazon, Microsoft)
  • Include logos, buttons, branded colors, layout tables
  • Some emails are Base64 encoded to hide contents
  • Makes phishing pages or emails visually convincing

9- Business Email Compromise (BEC)

  • Targets trust and workflows in business relationships
  • Often leads to:
    • Wire transfer fraud
    • Data leakage
    • Vendor payment rerouting
  • Methods include spoofing, compromised accounts, and zombie phishing (replying in old threads)

10- Lure Documents & Social Engineering Files

  • Invoices, salary forms, HR documents used as bait
  • Paired with impersonation or executive spoofing
  • Designed to appear routine to avoid suspicion
  • Some link to malicious downloads or harvest credentials
