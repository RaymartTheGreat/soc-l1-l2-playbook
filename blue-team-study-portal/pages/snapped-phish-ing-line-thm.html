Snapped Phish-ing Line (THM)

Scenario

As an IT department personnel of SwiftSpend Financial, one of your responsibilities is to support your fellow employees with their technical concerns. While everything seemed ordinary and mundane, this gradually changed when several employees from various departments started reporting an unusual email they had received. Unfortunately, some had already submitted their credentials and could no longer log in.

You now proceeded to investigate what is going on by:

**1. Analysing the email samples provided by your colleagues.

  1. Analysing the phishing URL(s) by browsing it using Firefox.
  2. Retrieving the phishing kit used by the adversary.
  3. Using CTI-related tooling to gather more information about the adversary.
  4. Analysing the phishing kit to gather more information about the adversary.**

https://tryhackme.com/room/snappedphishingline

we are provided with 5 emails

1- Who is the individual who received an email attachment containing a PDF?

William McClean

we can check each and look for pdf attachment but the faster way is to use grep grep "\.pdf” *

we got 1 email that has pdf attachment we open it to see the receiver of the email

2- What email address was used by the adversary to send the phishing emails?

Accounts.Payable@groupmarketingonline.icu

3- What is the redirection URL to the phishing page for the individual Zoe Duncan? (defanged format)

hxxp[://]kennaroads[.]buzz/data/Update365/office365/40e7baa2f826a57fcf04e5202526f8bd/?email=zoe[.]duncan@swiftspend[.]finance&error

we open zoe email and see it have a html attachment we download it and open it upon inspecting this html file we see its designed to redirect the user to phishing site

4- What is the URL to the .zip archive of the phishing kit? (defanged format)

hxxp[://]kennaroads[.]buzz/data/Update365[.]zip

the hint says to enumerate the url so we visit the domain we got from the previous question kennaroads.buzz we can see its a wordpress website and hosting some files go to /data we can see the zip file which is a phishing kit we download it for later analysis

5- What is the SHA256 hash of the phishing kit archive?

ba3c15267393419eb08c7b2652b8b6b39b406ef300ae8a18fee4d16b19ac9686

sha256sum

6- When was the phishing kit archive first submitted? (format: YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM:SS UTC)

2020-04-08 21:55:50 UTC

submit the hash to virustotal which confirm its a phishing kit you can find the answer in details section under history

7- When was the SSL certificate the phishing domain used to host the phishing kit archive first logged? (format: YYYY-MM-DD)

2020-06-25

didn’t see any ssl cert on the website so i checked the hint

8- What was the email address of the user who submitted their password twice?

michael.ascot@swiftspend.finance

we see in Q4 a log.txt file we download it using wget and we see it contain information about the victims who feel for the phishing

using grep we can see that micheal ascot email was submitted twice

9- What was the email address used by the adversary to collect compromised credentials?

m3npat@yandex.com

unzip the phishing kit from Q4 found submit.php which is a submit button that send email to m3npat@yandex.com

10- The adversary used other email addresses in the obtained phishing kit. What is the email address that ends in "@gmail.com"?

jamestanner2299@gmail.com

used grep to search for any @gmail.com in the directory

we see that phishing kit also send email to this email address with the yandex from the previous question

11- What is the hidden flag?

THM{pL4y_w1Th_tH3_URL}

tried to access /data/update365/office365 and it redirect us and from the hint (The flag contains a ".txt" extension and, with some adjustments, should be downloadable from the phishing URL. Look for the flag in every subdomain/directory of the phishing URL.)

so i just guessed flag.txt and it worked got the flag encoded with base64 and reversed
